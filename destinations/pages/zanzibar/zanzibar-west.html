West Zanzibar

Quiet coastal villages, historic sites, and local island life near Stone Town.

The Local Side of Zanzibar

West Zanzibar offers a grounded and authentic experience. Close to Stone Town yet far from crowds, this coastline reveals village life, hidden beaches, and stories rooted deep in Zanzibar’s history.

Bububu

Village life by the sea

Bububu Village

North of Stone Town

Bububu is a coastal village known for its relaxed atmosphere and connection to Zanzibar’s literary and cultural history. It offers a glimpse into everyday island life close to the city.

  • Authentic Swahili village atmosphere
  • Local fishing and coastal life
  • Historic associations with early writers
  • Easy access from Stone Town

Mangapwani

History beneath the surface

Mangapwani Coast & Caves

West Coast of Zanzibar

Mangapwani is known for its hidden caves and quiet beaches. Beneath its calm surface lies a powerful history connected to Zanzibar’s past and the Indian Ocean trade routes.

  • Historic caves and underground chambers
  • Quiet beaches and rocky coastline
  • Strong historical significance
  • Peaceful, uncrowded environment

Livingstone Beach

Hidden coastline and local escapes

Livingstone Beach

Near Mangapwani

Livingstone Beach is a secluded stretch of coastline surrounded by nature. It is a favorite local escape, offering calm waters, fresh air, and uninterrupted sea views.

  • Secluded and natural setting
  • Minimal development
  • Ideal for quiet beach time
  • Local picnic and relaxation spot
